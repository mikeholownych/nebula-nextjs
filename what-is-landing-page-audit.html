What Is a Landing Page Audit?

Let's clear up a common misconception.

A landing page audit is not someone's subjective opinion about whether your page looks good. "I don't like the font" or "the logo should be bigger" is not an audit — it's a design preference. An audit is a scored diagnostic that evaluates your page against objective conversion principles.

Think of it like a health checkup. You don't go to the doctor and ask "how do I feel?" You get blood work, a heart rate reading, and a cholesterol score. Each metric tells you something specific about a system that matters.

A landing page audit works the same way. It scores five independent dimensions — headline, CTA, social proof, speed, and mobile — on a 1-to-10 scale. The result is a composite score and, more importantly, a prioritized list of what to fix first.

Nebula's audit framework was built by analyzing thousands of landing pages across SaaS, e-commerce, and lead-gen verticals. The scoring is deterministic: the same page run twice produces the same result. There's no guessing, no "maybe try this" — just a data-backed answer to the question what is broken and how hard is it to fix?

What Are the 5 Dimensions a Landing Page Audit Should Score?

Not all audits are created equal. A checklist that just says "add a testimonial" is useless — it doesn't tell you which testimonial, where to put it, or what it should say. Nebula's framework goes deeper.

Here are the five dimensions that matter, in the order you should fix them:

1 Headline & Value Proposition

Your headline has roughly 3 seconds to tell a visitor they're in the right place. This dimension scores whether the headline communicates a specific outcome for a specific audience — or just describes what your product does. The most common failure: writing a headline that describes the product ("AI-Powered Analytics Dashboard") instead of the outcome ("Stop Losing Deals to Slow Reports").

2 Call-to-Action Clarity

A CTA like "Learn More" or "Get Started" leaves the visitor guessing what happens next. This dimension scores whether your button text names the specific action and outcome. The fix: "Get My Free Audit in 30 Seconds" converts 3x better than "Submit." Nebula's audit checks for vague CTAs, buried CTAs, and CTAs that require scrolling past 2,000 words of text.

3 Social Proof & Trust Signals

This dimension scores whether your page answers the unspoken question every visitor has: "Why should I trust you?" Mentions, testimonials, logos, case studies, number of users, and guarantees all count. The benchmark: You need at least one trust signal visible before the first CTA, ideally two. If your page is all copy and no proof, this score drops hard.

4 Page Speed & Performance

Google says 53% of mobile visits leave a page that takes longer than 3 seconds to load. This dimension scores Core Web Vitals — Largest Contentful Paint (LCP), First Input Delay (FID), and Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS). What we check: uncompressed images, render-blocking scripts, slow fonts, and excessive JavaScript that delays interactivity. Speed fixes are often the highest-impact, lowest-effort wins.

5 Mobile Experience

60% to 70% of B2B landing page traffic comes from mobile devices. This dimension scores whether your page is genuinely usable on a phone — not just "responsive" in the technical sense. Common fails: tiny tap targets, horizontal scroll, overlapping elements, forms that require desktop-level typing, and CTAs that hide below the fold.

Each dimension is scored independently because they require different fixes. A headline problem needs copy. A speed problem needs technical work. An audit that lumps everything into "fix your page" is worse than useless — it gives you no direction.

How Do You Interpret Landing Page Audit Results?

A landing page audit score is a number between 1 and 10. But what does that number actually mean? Here's a breakdown.

Score Range What It Means Next Step
1–3 Critical failures across multiple dimensions. The page is actively repelling buyers. Headline is unclear, CTA is buried or nonexistent, speed is poor, and mobile is broken. Audit → Fix Kit → apply the 3 easiest fixes today. The page needs a rebuild if the score is below 2.
4–6 The typical range for most pages. Some things work — headline might be okay, speed might be fine — but there are clear leaks. Usually 2–3 medium-impact fixes will move the needle. Audit → prioritize the fixes by difficulty → DIY the easy ones, consider the $147 pack for medium difficulty.
7–8 Above average. The fundamentals are solid. Headline communicates value, CTA is clear, trust signals are present. Improvements here are about polish and testing — incremental gains. Audit → pick 1–2 optimization opportunities → A/B test before committing to changes.
9–10 Rare. The page has been rigorously tested and optimized. This is what a post-CRO program page looks like — not a starting point. Focus on ad strategy, not page changes. You've already solved the conversion problem.

The most important number isn't the overall score — it's the dimension scores. A 6 overall might hide a speed score of 3 (urgent fix) and a CTA score of 8 (don't touch it). Always look at the individual dimension scores before deciding what to fix.

What a 4/10 Audit Looks Like

A score of 4 is the most common starting point. Here's what it typically means:

  • Headline (4/10): Describes the product, not the outcome. "Cloud-Based CRM Platform" instead of "Close 30% More Deals Without Adding Headcount."
  • CTA (3/10): "Learn More" or "Submit" — generic, low-commitment language that trains visitors to browse without buying.
  • Social Proof (2/10): Zero trust signals above the fold. No logos, no testimonials, no numbers. The page expects trust without earning it.
  • Speed (6/10): Loads in about 3 seconds on desktop, 5+ on mobile. Leavable but not terrible.
  • Mobile (5/10): Technically responsive but the CTA is tiny and the form requires desktop-level typing.

The fix order: Headline rewrite (30 min, copy only) → CTA change (10 min) → Add one trust signal above the fold (varies) → Speed optimization (technical, may need developer) → Mobile CTA resize (15 min).

What an 8/10 Audit Looks Like

An 8 is a page that's working but can be better. Example profile:

  • Headline (8/10): Specific outcome, audience-targeted. Minor polish opportunity in the subheadline.
  • CTA (9/10): Action-outcome language, high-contrast button, placed above the fold and repeated mid-page.
  • Social Proof (8/10): Logos present, one testimonial visible. Could use a second, more specific case study.
  • Speed (7/10): Good on desktop, acceptable on mobile. A few images could be compressed further.
  • Mobile (8/10): Usable and clean. Minor spacing tweak on the form.

The fix order here is different: you don't need big changes. You need A/B tests on the headline, a stronger testimonial, and image compression. These are optimization moves, not rescue moves.
