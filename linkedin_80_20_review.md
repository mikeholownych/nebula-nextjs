## Verdict

Useful, but not as a direct revenue engine.

This is an authority/inbound compounding play. It will not replace trigger-aware outbound for July 4 revenue. Use it to make the Business OS visibly inevitable and create future inbound for $97 audits and $1.5k-$5k SDR service.

## Core method

Cam's loop:

1. Find what is trending on fast platforms, especially X.
2. Translate it for LinkedIn, which moves slower.
3. Package it as Idea + Format + Angle.
4. Use proven post structures/swipe files instead of inventing from scratch.
5. Make technical material understandable to nontechnical buyers.
6. Write in a real voice with a clear opinion.
7. Engage 30 minutes/day.
8. Mix viral, authority, personal, and lead magnet posts weekly.

## What matters for Nebula

### Keep
- Bridge fast AI/business signals from X/HN/Reddit to LinkedIn.
- Translate technical agent work into founder/business outcomes.
- Use screenshots, simple AI graphics, and proof artifacts instead of polished design.
- Post same-day when possible for timeliness.
- Reply to every comment.
- Use lead magnets weekly.

### Ignore
- Follower-count worship.
- Pure viral posts with no buyer path.
- Generic AI news commentary.
- Content batching if it strips current context.
- Technical deep dives aimed at engineers instead of founders.

## Nebula content pillars

### 1. Growth / viral
Goal: reach.

Angles:
- "AI agents are replacing $5k/mo ops roles. Most founders are looking at the wrong metric."
- "The founder mistake: building more features when the landing page is leaking paid traffic."
- "LinkedIn is late to every AI operations shift. Here is what X/HN saw 48 hours ago."

### 2. Authority
Goal: trust.

Angles:
- Public teardown of a landing page conversion leak.
- Breakdown of trigger-aware lead scoring vs demographic scraping.
- Ledger-backed daily business OS decision memo.
- How autonomous agents classify weak/moderate/strong buyer signals.

### 3. Personal/build-in-public
Goal: connection.

Angles:
- "I am trying to build the first autonomous business with real revenue proof. Here is what broke today."
- "Revenue is still $0. The lesson is not 'AI failed.' The lesson is our targeting was a filter, not a trigger."
- "The uncomfortable part of autonomous business: agents can do work, but evidence discipline decides whether any of it matters."

### 4. Lead magnet
Goal: capture.

Angles:
- Free landing page leak audit.
- Ad burn leak checklist.
- Trigger-aware outreach scorecard.
- `Drop your landing page. I'll identify the first leak costing you conversions.`

## Weekly operating cadence

Minimum viable LinkedIn cadence:

- Monday: viral/trend bridge post
- Tuesday: authority teardown
- Wednesday: personal/build-in-public post
- Thursday: viral/trend bridge post
- Friday: lead magnet post
- Daily: 30 minutes commenting/replying/connecting

## KPIs that matter

Do not optimize for impressions alone.

Track:
- profile visits
- comments from founders/operators
- audit page clicks
- audit submissions
- checkout clicks
- paid conversions
- qualified inbound conversations

Classify signals:
- Weak: likes, generic praise, impressions
- Moderate: comment with specific pain, DM, audit question
- Strong: audit submission, checkout attempt, payment, referral

## Growth Agent prompt addition

Before each LinkedIn post, Growth should answer:

1. What fast-platform signal am I translating for LinkedIn?
2. Which buyer does this attract?
3. What business outcome does it tie to?
4. Is the language founder-readable? Mum Test pass/fail.
5. What is the CTA: comment, audit, checkout, or follow?
6. What metric will prove this post mattered?

## Recommended decision

Implement as a secondary compounding channel.

Priority:
1. Keep trigger-aware outbound as the revenue engine.
2. Add 5 LinkedIn posts/week as authority/inbound engine.
3. Require every lead magnet post to route to the audit page or checkout.
4. Ledger weekly outcomes. Kill or revise if 3 weeks produce no moderate/strong signals.
