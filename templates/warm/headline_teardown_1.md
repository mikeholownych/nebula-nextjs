Subject: {first_name}, a headline teardown (before and after)

{first_name},

We ran two versions of a headline for a SaaS client last month. Same product, same page, different framing.

**Before:**

> "Automated email marketing for SaaS teams"

Technically correct. Describes category. The visitor reads and thinks: "Okay, but what does that mean for me?"

**After:**

> "Get your trial users to convert without chasing them manually"

Same product. Different conversation. This one answers what they get, not what it is.

**What changed:**

1. **From category → outcome.** "Email marketing" = what it is. "Convert without chasing" = what they get.
2. **From feature → problem removal.** "Automated" = feature. The visitor infers the benefit. The new version names the pain directly.
3. **From general → specific.** "SaaS teams" = broad. "Trial users to convert" = the exact problem they're facing.

**The result:**

+31% click-through to trial start. Same traffic source. Same page below the fold. The only change: the headline.

**Why it worked:**

The visitor didn't have to translate. They saw a description of their problem, in their terms, with a clear outcome.
