Subject: {first_name}, your CTA needs to answer 5 questions

{first_name},

We see this pattern constantly: the button is visible, the color is tested, the placement is above the fold — but the click rate is flat.

The missing piece: the visitor doesn't know what the click commits them to.

**The 5 questions your CTA must answer:**

1. **What happens next?** (example: "Review your audit" vs. "Get started")
2. **How much time?** (example: "2-minute read" vs. nothing)
3. **Do I have to talk to someone?** (example: "No call required" vs. ambiguous)
4. **Will I be charged?** (example: "Free" vs. unclear)
5. **What do I get?** (example: "Your audit report" vs. "Get started")

**Test it:**

Read your CTA button. Then ask: does a stranger know the answer to all 5?

If not, they're not deciding. They're guessing.

**Example we tested:**

| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "Get started" | "Run my free audit (60 seconds)" |

+24% click-through. Same page. Same audience. Different clarity.

**Why it worked:**

The visitornow knows:
- What happens: audit runs
- How long: 60 seconds
- Cost: free
- No call: implied by self-serve format

No guessing.
