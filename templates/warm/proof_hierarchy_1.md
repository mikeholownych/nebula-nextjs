Subject: {first_name}, most testimonials are decoration. Proof requires hierarchy.

{first_name},

The audit found testimonials on your page. The question is: are they evidence or decoration?

**The evidence hierarchy (from weakest to strongest):**

| Level | Type | Believability |
|-------|------|---------------|
| 1 | Generic quote + name | Visitor dismisses as marketing |
| 2 | Specific result | Better — but still unverified |
| 3 | Name + photo + company | Real person, but outcome not shown |
| 4 | Specific result + link to source | **Verifiable. Skeptics pause.** |
| 5 | Video or screenshot | **Strongest. Hard to fake.** |

**What we typically see:**

> "Nebula helped us fix our landing page." — Jane D., CEO

This is Level 1. A stranger sees: marketing copy.

**What Level 4 looks like:**

> "We went from 1.2% to 3.8% trial conversion after fixing the headline + CTA. [See the case study](link)"
> — Jane Doe, CEO at [Company] — [LinkedIn](link)

Same quote. Different evidence weight.

**The test:**

Read each testimonial you have. Ask: can a stranger verify anything in this?

If the answer is no, it's decoration. It might still help. But it's not proof.
