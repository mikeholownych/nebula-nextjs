Subject: {first_name}, one campaign, two different conversations

{first_name},

We audited a landing page recently. The ad said: "Cut your CAC in half with smarter targeting."

The headline said: "The all-in-one analytics platform."

Both true. But they're not in the same room.

**The ad created an expectation:**

> I'll learn how to spend less acquiring customers.

**The headline introduced a different idea:**

> This is an analytics tool.

The visitor arrives expecting one conversation. The page starts a different one. They have to reconcile it.

Most don't. They bounce.

**What happened:**

- Ad team: optimized for clicks, found "smarter targeting" angle
- Page team: updated headline to match SEO target
- Campaign: shipped without anyone checking if they connected

**The fix:**

Not rewrite both. Pick one thread:

**Thread A (from ad):**

> Headline: "Cut your CAC by identifying which channels actually convert"
> Subhead: "See exactly where your best customers come from — and stop funding the others."

**Thread B (from page):**

> Ad: "See which channels drive your best conversions — not just clicks"
> Headline (unchanged): "The all-in-one analytics platform"

Either works. The leak is not the message itself. It's the mismatch.
