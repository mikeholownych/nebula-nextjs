Subject: {first_name}, your headline describes what it is, not what they get

{first_name},

We see this often in audits: the headline names the product category, but the visitor has to do the work to figure out if it's for them.

**What we found:**

{headline_text}

This is technically accurate. But it means the visitor has to answer three questions before they can act:

1. Is this for someone like me?
2. Does it solve the problem I actually have?
3. What happens if I click?

The headline doesn't answer any of them explicitly. The visitor infers.

**Why it happens:**

Most teams write from inside the product. They describe what they built. The visitor arrives from an ad that promised a specific outcome. The gap is where the leak is.

**One quick check:**

Read your headline. Then ask: would a stranger know exactly what they'll get? If the answer is "probably," they're guessing.
