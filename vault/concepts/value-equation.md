# Value Equation Framework

## Core Principle
The value equation has four variables arranged as a fraction:

**NUMERATOR (maximize these):**
- **Dream Outcome:** The specific, measurable result the prospect wants
- **Perceived Likelihood of Achievement:** How confident the prospect is that this offer will actually deliver

**DENOMINATOR (minimize these):**
- **Time Delay:** How long before they see results
- **Effort & Sacrifice:** How much work the prospect has to put in

## Implementation Rules
1. **Dream Outcome Upgrade:** Describe the result in the prospect's own language from the ICP Memo
2. **Likelihood Maximizer:** Use proof, guarantees, and case studies to increase confidence
3. **Time Compression:** Design a quick win that happens within first 48 hours
4. **Effort Reduction:** Eliminate, automate, or do-for-them the highest-friction steps

## Optimization Formula
To make an offer feel irresistible, increase the numerator and decrease the denominator. The bigger the gap between numerator and denominator, the more valuable the offer feels relative to its price.
