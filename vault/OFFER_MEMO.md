# OFFER_MEMO: Nebula Components B2B Offer Audit & Restructure

## Executive Summary
Nebula Components currently offers landing page audits and implementation services to startup founders struggling with ad spend and zero conversions. This memo documents the gap analysis between market needs (from ICP_MEMO) and current offer positioning, and provides a restructured offer aligned with the Value Equation framework.

## 1. WHAT THE MARKET WANTS (from ICP_MEMO)

**Trigger Event:** Log into ad account Monday morning. CTR fine, spend up, sales flat. Screenshot + post to Reddit.

**Bleeding Neck Problems:**
- "Spent $10k on Meta & Google Ads — barely any orders"
- "Google ads manager says he doesn't know what else to do"
- "Is Meta Ads getting worse in 2026 or am I doing something wrong?"
- "clicks but no sales, I have no idea what's broken"
- "landing page won't convert no matter what I change"

**Psychological Architecture:**
- **Silent Objection:** "The last person I paid said it was a testing phase for 3 months. I ended up with nothing and a lighter bank account."
- **Green Light Outcome:** Specific dollar number — "your page is currently losing ~$X/month based on your traffic and CTR." Once they see the number, $97 is trivial math.
- **Enemy:** Ad agency/freelancer who blames the algorithm and charges for a "testing phase"

**Insider Vocabulary:**
- message match
- above-fold conversion
- ROAS cliff
- hook-to-LP gap
- CPM creep
- offer clarity score

**Competitor Complaint Map:**
- Zamp: "want a 60-minute discovery call before pricing" / "no self-serve option"
- Hotjar/FullStory: "shows me where people click but not what to fix"
- Generic agencies: "built the page, ran the ads, blamed the niche" / "40-page PDF report, no prioritization"

**Blue Ocean Gap:** Nobody delivers specific diagnosis + dollar leak amount + fast self-serve implementation — all three together.

## 3. THE GAPS: Market Needs vs Current Offer

### Gap 1: Dollar Leak Specificity Missing
**Market Needs:** Explicit dollar amount — "your page is losing ~$X/month"
**Current Offer:** Generic "conversion leaks" with subjective scoring
**Impact:** Prospects can't do ROI math; trust stays low

### Gap 2: Silent Objection Not Addressed
**Market Needs:** Fear of "testing phase" agencies that charge and deliver nothing
**Current Offer:** No mention of agency avoidance or time-to-value guarantee
**Impact:** Prospects assume Nebula is another agency; hesitation remains

### Gap 3: Insider Vocabulary Underutilized
**Market Needs:** message match, ROAS cliff, hook-to-LP gap, CPM creep
**Current Offer:** Generic "clarity" and "trust gap" terms
**Impact:** Sophisticated buyers dismiss as basic; doesn't signal expertise

### Gap 4: Process Still Sounds Like Testing Phase
**Market Needs:** Fast, specific, self-contained diagnosis
**Current Offer:** Audit → Fix Pack → Implementation pipeline sounds multi-step
**Impact:** Triggers "another testing phase" alarm

### Gap 5: Green Light Outcome Not Explicit
**Market Needs:** "Once they see the number, $97 is trivial math"
**Current Offer:** "$97 Fix Pack" presented as separate product
**Impact:** Value-to-price bridge missing; price seems arbitrary

## 4. THE COMMODITY TEST

**Result:** Currently moderately commodity-positioned

**Why:** The offer structure (audit → fixes → implementation) mirrors hundreds of landing page tools and freelancers. The differentiation lies in the ICP-specific elements but these are not yet prominent in messaging.

**Specific Interchangeable Competitors:**
1. Landing page grader tools (Unbounce, Instapage, etc.)
2. Conversion rate optimization freelancers
3. Generic SaaS audit services
4. Marketing agency retainer packages

**Key Commonalities:**
- Free audit as lead magnet
- Tiered pricing (low/medium/high)
- Implementation options
- Conversion-focused

## 5. THE SILENT OBJECTION TEST

**#1 Silent Objection:** "Another agency that will charge for a testing phase and deliver nothing."

**Current Offer Addresses It?** Partially but not explicitly

**What's Missing:**
1. No specific time-to-value guarantee (e.g., "first fix within 2 hours")
2. No dollar-leak calculation to justify ROI
3. No mention of avoiding agency bullshit
4. No "done-for-you vs advice" distinction for self-sufficient founders

**Consequence:** The objection remains active during evaluation, reducing conversion, especially at higher price points ($147+).

**Gap:** 2/10 — outcome not quantified or tied to bleeding neck

**Current:** Implied fast (60 seconds for audit)
**Market Needs:** Same-day/same-hour fixes for urgent problem
**Gap:** 6/10 — good but could be tighter (2-hour guarantee vs 24h)
