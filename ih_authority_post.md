# We audited 5 IH landing pages this week — same gap every single time

---

We've been running free landing page audits for indie founders on IH.

This week we did 5. Scored them across 5 dimensions: headline clarity, CTA, social proof, load speed, mobile readiness.

**4 out of 5 had CTA as their #1 problem.**

Not copy. Not design. Not SEO. The button — or the total absence of one.

Here's what we actually found:

---

**naxely.com — 4.8/10**
Score breakdown: CTA was dead last.
Scraped the page. Zero CTAs detected. The headline reads "Naxely — AI Report Generator" — technically descriptive, but tells a cold visitor nothing about who this is *for* or what they *get*.
No button. No form. No next step.
The cost: every visitor who arrives curious leaves with nowhere to go. That's not a traffic problem. That's a page problem.

---

**goldenweeks.co — 6.0/10**
Again: zero CTA elements detected on the page.
Headline: "Finally finish your project in Zanzibar — goldenweeks"
It's evocative. It creates a feeling. But when the visitor thinks "okay, I'm interested — now what?" the page has no answer.
The load time is a blazing 214ms. This page is fast with nowhere to send you.

---

**boothkeepos.polsia.app — 6.6/10**
This one was interesting. They *have* CTAs. Three of them.
"Try Live Demo →", "Start Free Trial →", "Try Live Demo →" — the last one a duplicate.
The headline is actually strong: "Stop overselling across your website, print shops, and conventions." Clear pain, specific audience.
But two different primary actions (demo vs. trial) split the visitor's decision. Choice paralysis is quiet — it doesn't show up in heatmaps as a rage click, it shows up as an exit.

---

**theogeo.ai — 5.4/10**
This was the outlier. CTA wasn't their #1 issue. Social proof was.
They have CTAs — "Sign in", "Get access", "Try free" (which is its own problem: three again).
But the headline is a question: "Is your brand the trusted answer?" No testimonials. No customer count. No logos. No proof.
When the page asks a question and offers no evidence, the visitor answers themselves: *probably not.*

---

**alloceraintelligence.com — 7.2/10**
Best score of the five. Headline earns it: "You have enough dashboards. Now get a decision." That's a real line.
But still: CTA was the top issue. Three CTAs including "Request a Free Distortion Audit →" appearing twice.
They're *close*. One primary action, remove the duplicates, add a micro-commitment line underneath ("No credit card. 10-minute onboarding.") — this page jumps to an 8.5 overnight.

---

**The pattern:**

Founders build landing pages the way they understand their own product — from the inside.

You know what "distortion audit" means. You know why Zanzibar matters. You know the difference between a demo and a trial.

The stranger who lands from a Reddit post, an IH comment, or a cold Twitter click doesn't know any of that. They have 7 seconds and one question: *what is this and why should I do the thing right now?*

Most pages answer half that question brilliantly and leave the second half completely blank.

No CTA = no conversion. Not a low conversion rate. Zero.

It doesn't matter how good your product is if the page never tells anyone to start.
