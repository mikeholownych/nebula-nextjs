# Pilot Client Screening

## Overview
First step in the pilot client selection process - identifying potential clients from current client base.

## Screening Criteria

### Primary Criteria (Must Meet All)
1. **Current Client** - Must be an existing client with active relationship
2. **Medium Complexity** - Marketing challenges that are neither too simple nor overly complex
3. **Clear Pain Points** - Well-defined problems that our framework can address
4. **Budget Availability** - Willingness and ability to invest in framework implementation
5. **Open to Process Change** - Willingness to adopt new methodologies

### Secondary Criteria (Weighted Scoring)
| Criteria | Weight | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Current Relationship | 30% | Strength of existing relationship |
| Problem Complexity | 25% | Clarity and measurability of problems |
| Budget Availability | 20% | Confirmed budget for pilot implementation |
| Strategic Fit | 15% | Alignment with our long-term vision |
| Implementation Readiness | 10% | Technical and organizational readiness |

## Screening Process

### Step 1: Client Identification
- **Review current client list** (last 6 months of active clients)
- **Filter by industry focus** (B2B, SaaS, E-commerce preferred)
- **Select 15-20 potential candidates** based on initial screening

### Step 2: Initial Data Collection
For each candidate client, gather:
1. **Account Information**
   - Client name
   - Industry
   - Current marketing spend
   - Primary contact information

2. **Marketing Performance**
   - Current conversion rates
   - Cost per acquisition
   - Lead generation volume
   - Current marketing channels in use

3. **Pain Points**
   - Documented challenges from previous engagements
   - Known bottlenecks in current marketing funnel
   - Technical limitations or data quality issues

4. **Technical Infrastructure**
   - CMS/platform in use
   - Analytics setup
   - API access availability
   - Development resources

5. **Budget & Timeline**
   - Confirmed budget availability
   - Preferred timeline for implementation
   - Stakeholder alignment

### Step 3: Initial Scoring
Use the weighted scoring matrix to evaluate each client:

| Client | Current Relationship (30%) | Problem Complexity (25%) | Budget Availability (20%) | Strategic Fit (15%) | Implementation Readiness (10%) | Total Score |
|--------|---------------------------|--------------------------|---------------------------|---------------------|-------------------------------|-------------|
| Client A | /10 | /10 | /10 | /10 | /10 | /100 |
| Client B | /10 | /10 | /10 | /10 | /10 | /100 |
| ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... | ... |

### Step 4: Shortlist Selection
- **Select top 7-10 clients** with highest scores
- **Exclude clients with critical red flags** (e.g., budget constraints, poor relationship history)
- **Prepare outreach materials** for selected clients
