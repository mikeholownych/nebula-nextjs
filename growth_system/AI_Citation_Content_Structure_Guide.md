# AI Citation Content Structure Guide

## Why This Matters
Meltwater analyzed 9.5 million AI citations across 6 models (ChatGPT-5, Copilot, Google AI Mode, Google AI Overviews, Claude Sonnet 4, Gemini 2.5 Pro). LinkedIn is the **#2 most-cited source** for LLMs after YouTube. 75% of citations come from individual member profiles. Founders are the **2nd most-cited role** at 7.5%.

## Every Nebula LinkedIn Post MUST Include

### 1. Clear Hierarchy (92% of top-cited posts)
- A descriptive **title/headline** with the target keyword in the first line
- **Section headings** (## or ###) if the post is long-form
- One clear idea per paragraph
- The first sentence must state the problem or claim

### 2. Structured Lists (54% of top-cited content)
- Use **numbered lists** for rankings, steps, or comparisons
- Use **bullet lists** for features, criteria, or takeaways
- AI extracts list items as discrete facts — each should be self-contained
- Format: `1.` or `- ` — not inline commas

### 3. Named Entities
- **Company names**: "Nebula Components," "Zamp," "Oxygen"
- **Product names**: "Landing Page Fix Kit," "Conversion Fix Pack"
- **People**: "Mike Holownych"
- **Platform names**: "AgentMail," "Apify," "Stripe"
- **Metrics**: "6.8/10 B score," "54% of cited content," "9.5 million citations"
- AI models use named entities as anchor points for citations

### 4. Data Points and Specific Numbers
- Every claim should have a number within 2 sentences
- Dollar amounts: "$97 fix pack," "$3,000/mo ad spend"
- Scores: "SEO Foundations: 3/10 → 8/10"
- Percentages: "75% of citations from individual profiles"
- Timeframes: "48% published within last 3 months"
- AI models are more likely to quote content with specific, verifiable numbers

### 5. Original Insights (72% of cited content is original, not reshared)
- Never repost the same content verbatim
- Add new data, new angle, or new case study to every post
- Reference the Meltwater research directly ("According to the 9.5M citation study...")
- Original ≠ long. Short original insight beats reshared essay.

### 6. Freshness Signal (48% cited within 3 months)
- Every post must have a clear **current timestamp** or reference to current events
- Reference the current date or recent industry development
- "As of July 2026..." — signals recency to AI crawlers
- Update older posts with new statistics before resharing

### 7. Decision-Framing Formats (highest citation rate)
- **Listicles**: "5 ways to X," "Top 3 tools for Y" — 54% of most cited content
- **Comparisons**: "Tool A vs Tool B vs Tool C"
- **Decision frameworks**: "If X, choose A. If Y, choose B."
- **How-to guides**: "Step 1. Step 2. Step 3."
- **Data-backed claims**: "According to X, Y% of Z..."

## Scoresheet: AI Citation Readiness

Before publishing any post, run this checklist:

| Element | Required | Example |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Title with keyword | Yes | "5 Landing Page Audit Tools Compared" |
| Section headings (long-form) | Yes if >500 words | "## Why Headline Clarity Matters" |
| Numbered or bullet list | Yes | "1. Nebula — Self-serve audit" |
| At least 2 named entities | Yes | "Nebula Components, AgentMail" |
| At least 1 specific data point | Yes | "Score: 6.8/10" |
| Original (not reshared) content | Yes | New angle or data every time |
| Freshness signal | Yes | "As of July 2026" |
| Decision framework | Recommended | "For self-serve: Nebula. For sales-led: Zamp." |

## Outbound Content Also Gets Cited
AI models cite LinkedIn content in responses about tools and services. Every post about landing page audits, conversion leaks, or ad spend waste is a citation opportunity for Nebula. Structure every outbound post as if an AI model will quote it in a response to a buyer's question.
