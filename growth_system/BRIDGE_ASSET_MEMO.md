## PROMPT 1 OUTPUT: Concept Selection

### Gap Analysis (internal)

**Bleeding neck:** Monday morning. Ad account open. CTR: 4.2%. Spend: up 30% week-on-week. Conversions: 0. The founder screenshots the dashboard and posts "Is Meta getting worse or is it me?" They are not looking for a CRO agency. They are looking for a diagnosis.

**Mechanism gap:** They believe the problem is in the ad — targeting, creative, budget. The actual problem is the page. Specifically, the conversation the ad starts is interrupted the moment the visitor lands. The ad says "stop overpaying for SaaS tools." The landing page opens with "Welcome to Acme — trusted by 200 companies." The visitor leaves in 3 seconds. Not because the page is ugly. Because the mental contract was broken.

**Belief shift required:** "The ad was never the problem. My landing page is interrupting the conversation the ad started. Once I can see exactly where — and what it's costing me — the fix is obvious."

### Concept 1: Anti-Guide / Manifesto
**Hook:** "Why optimizing your ads made your conversion rate worse"
**Archetype:** Manifesto
**Bridge logic:** By attacking ad optimization as the default fix, the reader arrives at landing page message match as the root cause → Nebula diagnoses the exact gap.
**Depth:** Yes — requires explaining the psychology of ad-to-LP conversation continuity, why A/B testing headlines is the wrong lever, why CTR is a vanity metric when the page doesn't match.

### Concept 2: Self-Diagnostic
**Hook:** "The 5-minute landing page audit: find your ROAS cliff before you spend another dollar"
**Archetype:** Diagnostic
**Bridge logic:** They self-audit, find their gap, realize (a) the math is worse than they thought and (b) fixing it requires more than moving a button.
**Depth:** Yes — 5 dimensions with specific scoring criteria and dollar math.

### Concept 3: Proprietary SOP
**Hook:** "How we score a landing page in 47 minutes: the exact framework"
**Archetype:** Over-the-Shoulder
**Bridge logic:** Radical transparency builds trust. Reader sees the process, trusts the output, buys the audit.
**Depth:** Moderate.

### SELECTED: Concept 2 (Self-Diagnostic)
**Reason:** Highest bridge logic. The reader actively participates — they score their own page, see their own leak number, feel the gap themselves. The ICP can't look away from their own number. "Your page is scoring a 4.2/10 on message match and you're spending $8k/month" is not a pitch — it's a mirror.

**Working title:** "The ROAS Cliff: Why your CTR looks fine and your page converts nobody"

## PROMPT 2 OUTPUT: Persuasion Blueprint

**Archetype:** Diagnostic Audit (Analytical)
**Narrative engine:** From confused self-blame ("Is Meta getting worse or is it me?") → structured diagnosis → dollar-denominated reality → obvious next step
**Golden thread:** The ad isn't lying. The page is failing the conversation the ad already started.

### Chapter architecture

**Ch 1 (~600w): The screenshot that started it**
Hook: The CTR is fine. The spend is up. You have no sales. Here's what that actually means.
Transition: If the ad is working, what's failing?

**Ch 2 (~700w): Message match — the gap nobody talks about**
Hook: You've changed your headline 4 times. The conversion rate barely moved. Here's why.
Transition: Message match is the mechanism. Here's how to measure whether yours is broken.

**Ch 3 (~900w): The 5-dimension self-audit (the actual framework)**
Hook: This is the same scoring system we run on every page we audit. Run it yourself.
Transition: Your lowest score is your most expensive leak. Here's the dollar math.

**Ch 4 (~600w): The leak math — what a 0.4% conversion rate actually costs**
Hook: At $5k/month in ad spend, a 0.4% conversion rate and a 1.5% conversion rate is not a "10% improvement." It's 3.75x more revenue from the same traffic.
Transition: Once you can see the number, the fix decision is easy.

**Ch 5 (~500w): The 3 fixes that move the needle (not the 40-recommendation PDF)**
Hook: After 40+ page audits, the same 3 issues appear in 90% of them.
Transition: One of these applies to your page. You now know which one.

**Conclusion (~300w):** Bridge to free audit → inline $97 CTA.
