## DIAGNOSTIC REPORT (Prompt 1 — cynical buyer audit)

### What the market wants
From ICP_MEMO evidence table:
1. A dollar number on their specific leak — not "3.1 avg leak categories", a number like "$1,400/month"
2. A diagnosis, not a deliverable list — they want to know what's broken, not what's included
3. The silent objection addressed before they think it: "last person I paid did 3 months of testing and I got nothing"
4. Insider language: message match, hook-to-LP gap, above-fold conversion, ROAS cliff — not "CTA friction"
5. Zero effort path: paste URL, see result, buy fix right there, done

### What the offer currently says
- Headline: "Stop burning ad spend on a page that can't close"
- Promise: "Paste your URL. Get a scored teardown of exactly where your page leaks — clarity, CTA, trust proof, offer friction. In your inbox in 60 seconds. Free."
- Social proof: "Scored 40+ ad-funded landing pages · avg 3.1 leak categories per audit · avg reply <60 min, 24/7"
- Price ladder: $0 audit → $97 Fix Pack → $1,497 Growth Launch
- Risk reversal: "email us and say refund please" (buried in FAQ)

### The gaps

**Gap 1: No dollar number.**  
"avg 3.1 leak categories" is a process metric. The market's green light is a dollar amount. "Your page is leaking $X/month" is what makes $97 a non-decision. The current offer never shows the math. Fix: surface the estimated monthly leak in the audit output and in the above-fold copy.

**Gap 2: "Can't close" is not their language.**  
The ICP says "clicks but no sales, no idea what's broken" and "landing page won't convert no matter what I change." "Can't close" sounds like a sales coaching headline. Replace with their verbatim panic language.

**Gap 3: Silent objection not addressed above the fold.**  
"No sales call. No agency. No commitment." handles surface objections. The real block — "I've been burned by a testing phase" — never appears. It's the reason they hesitate at $97. It belongs in the first scroll, not an FAQ.

**Gap 4: Insider vocabulary missing from offer.**  
The offer uses "CTA friction, trust gap, offer friction" — internal audit dimensions. The ICP uses "message match, hook-to-LP gap, ROAS cliff." Using their vocabulary signals you understand their problem. The current offer sounds like a tool. It should sound like someone who ran paid traffic and knows exactly where it breaks.

**Gap 5: $97 purchase is disconnected from audit delivery.**  
The audit arrives by email. The buy decision happens separately. There is no inline "fix this now" CTA at the moment of maximum pain (when they're reading the audit and seeing their leaks). That's the highest-intent moment in the funnel — currently abandoned.

**Gap 6: Commodity test — FAIL.**  
"Scored teardown of 5 dimensions" sounds like Hotjar + a checklist. Nothing in the above-fold copy distinguishes Nebula from any other audit tool. The differentiation is (a) dollar-denominated diagnosis, (b) self-serve $97 same-day implementation, (c) no calls ever. None of these are in the first 200 pixels.

**Gap 7: Proof is volume, not identity-matched.**  
"40+ pages" is a count. The ICP needs to see a page like theirs — same vertical, same problem, same budget — get fixed. "We audited a Shopify founder running $8k/month in Meta spend. Above-fold message match gap. Fixed in 45 minutes. CTR-to-conversion went from 0.4% to 1.9%." That's identity-matched proof.

### Verdict
The offer is technically correct but strategically quiet. It describes the tool, not the transformation. It uses process language where it needs to use panic language. The path from free audit to $97 purchase has a gap at the highest-intent moment. The silent objection is never named. At $97 the price is already trivial math — the barrier is trust, not cost. The offer needs to earn that trust above the fold before any CTA appears, and it needs to show the dollar leak number so the $97 purchase feels like an obvious trade.
