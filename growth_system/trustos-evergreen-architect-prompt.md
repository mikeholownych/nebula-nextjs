# TrustOS Evergreen Email Architect — Prompt Reference
## Saved 2026-07-07 from https://trustos.flagstad.io/resources/evergreen-email-architect/

### Core Philosophy
- 20-email evergreen nurture loop, 1/week, loops email 20→1
- NOT a broadcast newsletter — each email is a peer-to-peer insight
- Consumption-first: teach, don't pitch (pitch only in P.S.)
- Requires 3 pre-built inputs: ICP_MEMO, OFFER_MEMO, LEAD_MAGNET

### The 4-Part Email Anatomy
1. **Hook** — external perspective or market pattern (not clickbait, not "you are wrong")
2. **Micro-dose** — ONE complete insight from lead magnet, fully taught in-body (no teasers)
3. **Bridge** — connect to full lead magnet asset (why the full guide has more)
4. **P.S. Sales Door** — ONLY sales ask in the email, one sentence, one link

### Ego Protection Protocol (Critical)
- NEVER accuse reader directly. "You are failing at X" → triggers defensiveness.
- Frame as market pattern: "We see a lot of companies struggle with X."
- Use third-party examples: "A client of ours was facing X."
- Position yourself as helpful observer, not judge.

### Voice Rules (Non-Negotiable)
- Forbidden words: leverage, unlock, unleash, harness, transformative, synergy, robust, seamless, game-changing, navigate, deep dive
- No metaphors (tapestries, symphonies, journeys)
- No em-dashes — rephrase or use commas
- No "Topic: Explanation" colon format
- Imperfect rhythm: mix 2-word sentences with long ones; vary paragraph lengths
- All lowercase subject lines except first letter
- No "we call this X" — don't invent names for concepts
- No "most people X" structure
- Read like a colleague sharing a thought over coffee, not a newsletter from a company

### Output Format Per Email
```
Subject Line Options (max 6 words, lowercase):
1. [low hype]
2. [curiosity]
3. [benefit]

Email Body:
Hi {firstname}

(Hook — external/market perspective)

(Micro-dose lesson)

(Bridge — pivot to lead magnet asset)

(CTA — link to lead magnet)

P.S.: (Sales door — link to offer/booking, one sentence, one link)
```

### Scheduling Rules
- One email per week (faster = higher unsubscribe)
- Tuesday/Wednesday morning best for B2B
- 20 emails → loop to 1 (5-month gap between repeats)
- Supplementary layers: pre-call belief emails (on booking), lead scoring (on engagement)
